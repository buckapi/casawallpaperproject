Installation Types

What’s the difference?
Learn more about installation

Traditional / Unpasted

Requires paste application. Strongest bond and best for high-moisture areas.

Advantages

  • Strongest long-term adhesion.
  • Excellent for bathrooms and humid areas.
  • Professional-grade finish.
  • Wide variety of premium wallpaper options.

Considerations

  • Requires wallpaper paste.
  • Installation takes longer.
  • Usually requires professional installation.

Best For

Permanent installations, luxury projects, bathrooms, kitchens, and commercial spaces.

Pre-Pasted

Paste is already on the back and activated with water. Faster and cleaner installation.

Advantages

  • Cleaner installation process.
  • No additional paste required.
  • Good balance between convenience and durability.
  • Suitable for most residential projects.

Considerations

  • Requires water activation.
  • Less forgiving during repositioning.
  • May not perform as well in high humidity.

Best For

Living rooms, bedrooms, offices, and homeowners looking for easier installation.

Peel & Stick

Self-adhesive backing. Easy DIY installation and removal.

Advantages

  • Fast DIY installation.
  • No paste or water required.
  • Easy removal with minimal wall damage.
  • Perfect for renters.

Considerations

  • May not adhere well to textured walls.
  • Not ideal for high-humidity environments.
  • Generally less durable than traditional wallpaper.

Best For

Apartments, temporary decor, rental properties, and DIY projects.

Wallpaper Materials

Which material is best for me?
See all materials guide

Vinyl / Non-Woven

Durable, washable and moisture resistant. Ideal for kitchens, bathrooms and high-traffic areas.

Advantages

  • Water-resistant and washable.
  • Very durable and long-lasting.
  • Easy maintenance.
  • Excellent for families with children or pets.

Considerations

  • Can be slightly more expensive.
  • Less natural texture than fiber-based materials.

Best For

Kitchens, bathrooms, hallways, laundry rooms, rental properties and busy households.

Grasscloth

Natural fibers that add warmth and rich texture to any space.

Advantages

  • Premium natural appearance.
  • Unique texture and depth.
  • Adds warmth and sophistication.
  • Each roll has a distinctive character.

Considerations

  • Not washable.
  • Visible seams are normal.
  • Color variations occur naturally.

Best For

Dining rooms, offices, luxury living rooms, feature walls and upscale interiors.

Paper

Classic and versatile with endless design options and finishes.

Advantages

  • Large variety of colors and patterns.
  • Affordable option.
  • Traditional wallpaper appearance.
  • Excellent print quality.

Considerations

  • Less resistant to moisture.
  • Can tear more easily during installation.
  • Not ideal for bathrooms.

Best For

Bedrooms, living rooms, dining rooms and decorative accent walls.

Textile / Fabric

Luxurious look and feel with fabric-based wallcoverings.

Advantages

  • Luxury designer appearance.
  • Rich texture and depth.
  • Can improve room acoustics.
  • Creates a sophisticated atmosphere.

Considerations

  • Usually higher cost.
  • Requires professional installation.
  • More delicate than vinyl materials.

Best For

Luxury bedrooms, boutique hotels, executive offices and elegant living spaces.
